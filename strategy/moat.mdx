## Three Engines of Lock-In

### 1. Money Gravity â€” Oruon

<Card title="The Wallet Moat" icon="vault">
  **Oruon becomes indispensable by centralizing financial operations:**
  - Complete transaction history for tax compliance

#### Switching Cost Analysis

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Financial History">
    **What you lose:**
    - Years of transaction records
    - Tax-ready statements
    - Audit trails
    - Reconciliation history

    - Manual export and reformatting
    - Re-categorization for tax purposes
    - Loss of historical trends
    - Accounting overhead

    **What you lose:**
    - Automated distribution logic
    - Partner agreements encoded in system
    - Historical split performance

## Ethical Stance on Lock-In

### Data Portability

<Accordion title="What We Support">
  - **Wallet data export:** Complete transaction history in CSV/JSON
  - **Statement downloads:** Tax-ready formats
  - **Media vault export:** Original files with metadata
  - **Evidence pack export:** Takedown documentation
  - **API access:** Programmatic data retrieval (enterprise tier)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Our Philosophy">
  We support data portability not despite lock-in, but **because** our defensibility comes from:
  - **Indispensable utility:** Features that are genuinely valuable
  - **Verified histories:** Time-stamped records difficult to recreate
  - **Network effects:** Integrated ecosystem that compounds value
  - **Continuous innovation:** Always providing new value

  We **don't** rely on:
  - Dark patterns or hidden friction
  - Proprietary data formats
  - Artificial export limitations
  - Contractual restrictions
</Accordion>

## Competitive Positioning

### vs. Pure Fintech

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Our Advantage">
    - **Ops layer:** Financial data drives intelligent operations
    - **Vault integration:** Protection justifies premium positioning
    - **Creator-specific:** Purpose-built for content creator needs
    - **Ecosystem play:** Not just payments, entire operations
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Their Advantage">
    - Lower transaction fees (commodity service)
    - More currency pairs and regions
    - Established banking relationships
    - Regulatory experience
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Our Moat">
    We **add** operations and protection layers that pure fintech can't easily replicate without rebuilding entire ecosystem.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### vs. Creator Tools

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Our Advantage">
    - **Wallet gravity:** Financial data they don't have
    - **Integrated signals:** Cross-product intelligence
    - **System of record:** Authoritative data they must integrate with
    - **Switching costs:** High friction to migrate away
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Their Advantage">
    - Feature depth in specific areas
    - Established user bases
    - Platform partnerships
    - Content format specialization
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Our Moat">
    We **own** the financial layer which they must integrate with or complement. Hard for them to backward integrate into fintech.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### vs. Platform-Native Tools

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Our Advantage">
